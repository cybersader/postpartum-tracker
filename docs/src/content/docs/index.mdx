## Why Postpartum Tracker?

Tracking feedings, diapers, medications, and recovery during the postpartum period is critical but overwhelming. Most tracking apps lock your data in proprietary formats and require constant connectivity.

Postpartum Tracker stores everything as JSON inside your Obsidian markdown notes. Your data is yours, always accessible, and never leaves your vault.

## Features

<CardGrid stagger>
  <Card title="Core tracking" icon="pencil">
    Feeding (breast/bottle with live timer), diaper changes (wet/dirty with stool color), and medication schedules (dose timers, risk bars, alternating alerts).
  </Card>
  <Card title="Tracker library" icon="list-format">
    20+ tracking modules: sleep, pumping, pain, mood, temperature, walking, tummy time, measurements, notes, and more. Enable what you need in settings.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Quick entry" icon="rocket">
    Type "fed left 20 min" or "wet diaper" in natural language. The built-in parser understands feedings, diapers, medications, sleep, and more -- with time and duration extraction.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Fully customizable" icon="setting">
    Configure each button's visibility, label, and icon. Choose timer animation colors. Reorder sections with drag-and-drop. Adjust hold-for-details behavior per tracker.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Smart notifications" icon="warning">
    In-app toasts, system notifications, and webhooks for external services. Feeding reminders, medication dose alerts, and interval-based reminders for library trackers.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Todoist integration" icon="external">
    Proactive tasks after logging, alert tasks when reminders fire, and two-way sync that creates tracker entries when you complete Todoist tasks.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Markdown native" icon="document">
    All data stored as JSON inside a markdown code block. No external database, no cloud dependency. Your data stays in your vault.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Mobile first" icon="laptop">
    Designed for one-handed operation with large touch targets, haptic feedback, and quick-action buttons. Works on desktop too.
  </Card>
